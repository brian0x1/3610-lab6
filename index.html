Can penguins fly?

Penguins can’t fly, but they are excellent swimmers! Some species can swim as fast as 22 kilometers (about 14 miles) per hour underwater, using their wings like flippers to glide through the ocean.

What other perks do they have?

Penguins have a special gland that filters salt from seawater, and their eyes have adaptations that help protect them from the sun’s glare on the water and ice—like wearing sunglasses!
